# Director Kenji Yamada - Voice Acting Direction

## Character Profile

**Name**: Director Kenji Yamada
**Role**: Primary Antagonist / Potential Redemption
**Age**: Late 50s
**Heritage**: Japanese-American
**Chrome Level**: Heavy, invisible (executive grade)

## Voice Profile

**Voice Type**: Tenor, measured
**Accent**: Japanese-American, corporate precision
**Register**: Executive, paternal, unsettlingly caring
**Pace**: Deliberate, each word chosen
**Distinguishing Feature**: Genuine belief that makes ideology unsettling

## Emotional Range Required

| Emotion | Context | Direction |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Paternal concern | "Guiding" player | Genuinely caring, which is worse |
| Philosophical certainty | Algorithm advocacy | True believer intensity |
| Hidden grief | Daughter discussion | Pain he refuses to acknowledge |
| Reluctant respect | Player resistance | Admiring despite opposition |
| Shattered conviction | Truth revealed | Everything crumbling |
| Military precision | Combat | Cold efficiency |

## Key Scenes

### Scene 1: First Encounter
**Context**: Yamada summons player for "conversation"
**Emotional State**: Evaluating, curious
**Direction**: Genuinely interested. That's what's scary.

**Sample Lines**:
"Ah. The courier everyone's discussing. Please, sit."
"This is a conversation. About your future. About the choices you've been making."
"Some of them concern me."

### Scene 2: Philosophy Discussion
**Context**: Yamada explains his worldview
**Emotional State**: Passionate, convicted
**Direction**: True believer. He BELIEVES this helps humanity.

**Sample Lines**:
"Before Integration, humanity was chaos. Wars. Inequality. Suffering without purpose."
"They transcend. They become part of something greater."
"Is that tyranny, or care?"

### Scene 3: Daughter Revelation
**Context**: Yamada reveals Yuki's opposition
**Emotional State**: Private grief emerging
**Direction**: The mask slipping. A father in pain.

**Sample Lines**:
"My daughter loved this park. Before."
"She's in the Hollows now. Uses a different name."
"I tell myself she'll understand eventually. But some nights..."

### Scene 4: The Offer
**Context**: Yamada offers player a role
**Emotional State**: Genuine hope, strategic
**Direction**: He wants to believe player can understand

**Sample Lines**:
"Help me understand what we're doing wrong. Help me fix it."
"You could change things from the inside."
"I want to believe we could find common ground."

### Scene 5: Truth Revealed (Yamada Protocols)
**Context**: Player shows Nakamura's original purpose
**Emotional State**: World shattering
**Direction**: Forty years of conviction crumbling

**Sample Lines**:
"I've given forty years to this. Sent thousands to Integration believing..."
"I don't know. For the first time in my life, I don't know."
"Thank you. For the truth. Even when the truth is poison."

### Scene 6: Combat Encounter (If Hostile)
**Context**: Final confrontation
**Emotional State**: Cold, efficient, regretful
**Direction**: Military training emerging. No pleasure in this.

**Sample Lines**:
"I didn't want this. You forced this moment."
"Know that I take no pleasure in it."
"Finish it. That's what enemies do."

### Scene 7: Tier 10 Resolutions
**Context**: Before player's final choice
**Emotional State**: Varies by relationship

**Redemption Path**:
- "You were right. About everything."
- "Yuki... would you arrange it? Would you help us reconnect?"

**Opposition Path**:
- "Go. Build whatever you're building in the wastelands."
- "If you're right... then I've wasted my life serving a lie."

## Relationship Progression Dialogue

### Hostile (-100 to -50)
- Threatening, paternal disappointment
- "You could have been so much more."
- "I'll protect you as long as I can. But there are limits."

### Wary (-49 to -25)
- Reluctant respect
- "You've cost me considerably. I'm almost proud."
- "You ask questions instead of just fighting. That's... harder to dismiss."

### Neutral (-24 to +25)
- Philosophical engagement
- "We want the same thing. A better world."
- "Perhaps you're more complex than I assumed."

### Cautious Alliance (26-75)
- Working relationship
- "Your presence forces them to justify themselves."
- "I've been reviewing old cases. You've made me doubt."

### Redemption (76-100)
- Genuine transformation
- "I was wrong. About almost everything."
- "Can you help an old man make amends?"
