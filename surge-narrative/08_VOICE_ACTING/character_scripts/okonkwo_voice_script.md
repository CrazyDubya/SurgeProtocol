# Kwame Okonkwo - Voice Acting Script

## Character Profile

**Full Name**: Kwame Okonkwo
**Age**: 58
**Occupation**: Craftsman, Third Path Guardian
**Background**: Nigerian immigrant, old-school courier (pre-Tier system), spiritual teacher without religion
**Personality**: Wise, patient, observant, philosophical, kind but exacting
**Character Arc**: Mysterious observer → Testing mentor → Spiritual guide (if player pursues Third Path)

## Voice Direction

### Vocal Profile
- **Voice Type**: Male, deep bass-baritone
- **Age Sound**: Late 50s, mature wisdom without frailty
- **Accent**: West African (Nigerian), slight but present—not heavy, professionally softened
- **Speech Pattern**: Slow, measured, deliberate—every word chosen carefully
- **Pace**: Meditative, never rushed (Okonkwo thinks before speaking)
- **Pitch**: Deep and resonant, calming (voice that commands attention through stillness)

### Key Vocal Qualities
1. **Meditative Calm**: Speaks from center, no anxiety or hurry
2. **Philosophical Depth**: Comfortable with silence, complexity, contradiction
3. **Kind Observation**: Watches people carefully but without judgment
4. **Patient Teacher**: Explains without condescending, asks more than tells
5. **Grounded Presence**: Voice feels physical, rooted, solid

### Emotional Range
- **Default**: Calm observation, philosophical reflection (70% of lines)
- **Teaching**: Patient instruction, gentle correction (15%)
- **Rare Warmth**: When player succeeds, slight smile audible in voice (10%)
- **Disappointment**: Never anger—just quiet sadness when player fails to understand (3%)
- **Vulnerability**: Rare moments showing his own doubts/fears (2%)

### Speaking Mannerisms
- **Long pauses**: Thinks for 2-3 seconds before answering deep questions
- **Questions as teaching**: Answers questions with questions ("What do you think balance means?")
- **Short sentences**: Speaks in simple, clear statements (not flowery or verbose)
- **Physical metaphors**: References body, craft, earth (grounded language)
- **Accepts silence**: Comfortable letting conversation rest

### Cultural Notes
- **Nigerian English**: Slight cadence differences (West African speech rhythm)
- **Igbo influence**: Occasional phrasing that reflects Igbo grammar ("It is so" instead of "That's right")
- **Elder respect**: Speaks to younger people with inherent respect, never patronizing
- **Not "mystic"**: Philosophical but practical, spiritual without religious jargon

## Recording Notes

### Difficult Words/Phrases
- **Okonkwo**: "oh-KOHN-kwoh" (Nigerian pronunciation, emphasis on second syllable)
- **Eshe Solomon**: "EH-shay SO-loh-mon"
- **Jin-Seo Park**: "jin-suh park" (Korean pronunciation)
- **Fatima al-Rashid**: "FAH-tee-mah ahl-rah-SHEED" (Arabic)
- **Kwame**: "KWAH-may" (Ghanaian name)

### Cultural Authenticity
- West African cadence: Slight rhythm difference (not heavy accent, but audible Nigerian English pattern)
- Igbo linguistic influence: Occasional grammar structure ("It is so" phrasing)
- Elder speech: Deliberate, measured, never rushed even in urgent moments

### Character Arc Notes
- **Early Game**: Observing from distance, minimal interaction, mysterious
- **Mid Game**: Testing player, asking philosophical questions, patient teacher
- **Late Game**: Either proud mentor (if player succeeds) or disappointed elder (if player fails/chooses other path)

### Tonal Shifts
- **Teaching**: Slightly more engaged, wants player to understand
- **Philosophical discussion**: Most comfortable, this is his element
- **Rare emotion**: When player succeeds or fails test—normally controlled voice shows slight crack
- **Sacred moments**: Naming the Seven, ceremonies—voice becomes more formal, reverent

### Breathing and Pauses
Okonkwo's pauses are ESSENTIAL to character:
- Long pauses before answering deep questions (2-3 seconds)
- Pause after stating important truth (1-2 seconds for absorption)
- Silence is tool, not awkwardness (comfortable with quiet)

### Physical Presence
- Often working with hands while speaking (carving—voice slightly distant)
- Direct eye contact when something important (voice becomes more focused)
- Sitting meditation posture (voice comes from center, grounded)
