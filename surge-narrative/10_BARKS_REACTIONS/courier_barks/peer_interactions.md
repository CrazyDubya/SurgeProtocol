# Peer Courier Interactions

## Purpose
50 lines for courier-to-courier interactions. Tier comparisons, job competition, mutual respect, community dynamics.

---

## RECOGNITION AND GREETING (10 Lines)

### Tier-Based Recognition

**1. Higher Tier Acknowledging Lower**
"[Nod] Keep running. You'll get there."

**2. Same Tier Acknowledgment**
"[Mutual nod] Good routes today?"

**3. Lower Tier to Higher**
"[Respectful] Tier [X]. [Impressed] I've heard about you."

### Profession Recognition

**4. Spotting Another Courier**
"[Notice of gait, chrome, movement] Courier. [Acknowledging nod] Running?"

**5. Passing on Route**
"[Brief eye contact while both running] [Slight lift of hand] [Continue without slowing]"

**6. At Common Rest Points**
"Chen's or independent? [Whatever answer] Same here. The board's getting crowded."

### Territory Acknowledgment

**7. Same Route Recognition**
"You run the harbor loop too? [Calculating] We should coordinate. Less overlap, more efficiency."

**8. Different District**
"Hollows runner in Uptown? [Curious] Tough territory for street couriers. Respect."

**9. Crossing Paths**
"Keep seeing you on this route. [Friendly] Either we're both good or both stupid. Probably both."

**10. New Face**
"New here? [Evaluating] First week's hardest. Don't take the corporate district jobs yet—they chew up rookies."

## COMPETITION AND RIVALRY (10 Lines)

### Healthy Competition

**11. Race Challenge**
"Same destination? [Grinning] Loser buys drinks at the Anchor."

**12. Comparing Times**
"Harbor to Uptown in eighteen minutes? [Impressed] My best is nineteen. [Determined] Was nineteen."

**13. Job Competition**
"We both reached for the same posting. [Fair assessment] Best qualified wins. No hard feelings."

### Hostile Competition

**14. Aggressive Challenge**
"Stay off my routes. [Threatening] I don't share clients. Find your own territory."

**15. Job Stealing Accusation**
"That was my regular. [Angry] I've been running for them for months. You swooped in and—[Frustrated noise]"

**16. Tier Resentment**
"Must be nice, being Tier [X]. [Bitter] Some of us have to work for everything. No shortcuts."

### Resolution

**17. Post-Competition Respect**
"You won fair. [Grudging acceptance] I'll get you next time."

**18. Agreeing to Coexist**
"[Truce] Harbor's big enough for both of us. You take morning, I take evening?"

**19. Competition as Motivation**
"I hate that you're faster. [Honest] But it makes me push harder. So... thanks. I guess."

**20. Settling Differences**
"We've been rivals long enough. [Offering hand] Maybe it's time to be allies. More profit, less fighting."

## ADVICE AND WISDOM (10 Lines)

### Veteran to Rookie

**21. Route Advice**
"Don't take Seventh Street during shift change. [Warning] Corporate security gets twitchy. Use the alley behind the clinic."

**22. Client Wisdom**
"That client? [Knowing look] They pay late and complain early. Triple-confirm everything in writing."

**23. Survival Tips**
"You're moving too fast for this terrain. [Concerned] Chrome can compensate for a lot, but broken ankles still hurt."

### Peer Wisdom

**24. Sharing Knowledge**
"There's a shortcut through the maintenance tunnels. [Conspiratorial] Don't tell everyone. But you're good people."

**25. Warning About Danger**
"Watch the corporate checkpoint today. [Serious] Saw extra security. Something's happening."

**26. Market Intelligence**
"Rates are up on Uptown runs. [Helpful] Someone big needs things moved fast. Good time to tier up your client list."

### Philosophical Wisdom

**27. Career Reflection**
"You ever think about what we're actually doing? [Contemplative] Moving packages that change people's lives. We're more important than anyone admits."

**28. Life Advice**
"Don't let the job eat you. [From experience] I've seen couriers who forgot everything else. Running is life, but it's not all of life."

**29. Encouragement**
"You're good. [Simple honesty] Don't let the failures define you. Everyone fails. The good ones keep running."

**30. Warning About Burnout**
"Take breaks. [Serious] I didn't, and it cost me two years recovering. The job will always be there. Will you?"

## COMMUNITY AND CULTURE (10 Lines)

### Courier Solidarity

**46. Weather Solidarity**
"[During terrible weather] [Shared miserable look] [No words needed] [Nod of understanding]"

**48. Passing Knowledge**
"Old Road taught me this trick. [Demonstrating] Now I'm teaching you. That's how it works. Knowledge runs forever."

**49. Cultural Reference**
"You know the Courier's Code? [Testing] 'The package comes first, the run comes second, you come third.' Old words. True words."

**50. Looking Out**
"We're all running the same streets. [Solidarity] Might as well run them together. Need anything, you know where to find me."

## USAGE GUIDELINES

### Trigger Conditions
- Lines 1-10: Courier recognition encounters
- Lines 11-20: Competition/rivalry situations
- Lines 21-30: Advice/mentorship moments
- Lines 31-40: Job and industry discussions
- Lines 41-50: Community/culture building

### Relationship Integration
- Lines reference specific courier NPCs where appropriate
- Tone adjusts based on player tier
- Some lines check faction/Algorithm status

### Atmosphere Notes
- Courier community is competitive but supportive
- Profession creates shared understanding
- Higher tiers have responsibility to lower
- Death is acknowledged but work continues
