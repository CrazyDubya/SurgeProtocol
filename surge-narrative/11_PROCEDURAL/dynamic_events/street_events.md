# Street-Level Dynamic Events

## Purpose
20 random street encounters that can occur during courier runs. These events add variety, create opportunities, and make the world feel alive. All events are tier-gated to ensure appropriate challenge and context.

---

## EVENT CATEGORIES

1. **Danger Events** (6) - Threats requiring response
2. **Opportunity Events** (5) - Chances to gain advantage
3. **Witness Events** (4) - Things the player sees (moral choice optional)
4. **Social Events** (5) - Interactions with NPCs

---

## DANGER EVENTS

### EVENT D1: MUGGING ATTEMPT

**Tier Range**: 0-4
**Trigger**: Random during transit, higher in Hollows
**Chance**: 8% base, modified by time of day

**Setup**:
*An alley you've passed a hundred times. This time, figures emerge from shadows. They want what you're carrying.*

**Muggers** (2-3):
- "Nice package. Hand it over, courier."
- "Nothing personal. Just business."
- "Don't try to run. We know these streets."

**Player Options**:
1. **Comply**: Lose package, fail job, reputation hit. Safe.
2. **Fight**: Combat encounter. Win = keep package. Lose = package taken.
3. **Bluff**: [Skill check] "You know who I work for?" Success = they back off.
4. **Run**: [Speed check] Success = escape with package. Fail = caught, harder fight.
5. **Bribe**: Offer credits to pass. Costs 50-100 credits.

**Outcomes**:
- Victory: +Reputation (word spreads you can't be robbed)
- Compliance: -Reputation, job failure
- Successful bluff: No change, muggers remember your face
- Escape: +Reputation for speed

**Scaling**:
Tier 0-1: 2 muggers, easy combat
Tier 2-3: 2-3 muggers, moderate combat
Tier 4: 3 muggers with chrome, difficult combat

### EVENT D2: CORPORATE CHECKPOINT

**Tier Range**: 2-6
**Trigger**: Transit through corporate-adjacent areas
**Chance**: 12% base, higher during "security alerts"

**Setup**:
*Checkpoint ahead. Corporate security scanning everyone. Your package may not pass inspection.*

**Security Officer**:
- "Step forward. Package on the scanner."
- "Routine security. Comply and you'll be on your way."
- "Any contraband to declare?"

**Player Options**:
1. **Comply**: Package scanned. Legal items = pass. Illegal = confiscation.
2. **Detour**: [Navigation check] Find alternate route. Time cost.
3. **Bribe**: [Persuasion] Offer "facilitation fee." 100-200 credits.
4. **Credentials**: [If player has contacts] "I'm expected at [executive name]'s office."
5. **Wait**: Sometimes checkpoints close. Wait 10-20 minutes.

**Outcomes by Package Type**:
- Legal: Pass through, minor delay
- Gray market: 50% flagged, lose package or bribe
- Illegal: Definitely flagged, major consequences

**Scaling**:
Higher tiers face more thorough inspection but have more bypass options.

### EVENT D3: GANG TERRITORY DISPUTE

**Tier Range**: 1-5
**Trigger**: Transit through contested areas
**Chance**: 6% base, modified by faction state

**Setup**:
*Chrome Saints and another faction are facing off ahead. The street is about to become a battlefield.*

**Environmental Description**:
*Tension crystallizes. Hands move toward weapons. Someone's going to make a move. The delivery route goes right through the middle.*

**Player Options**:
1. **Wait it out**: Find cover, wait for resolution. Time cost.
2. **Push through**: [Speed/Stealth] Run the gauntlet before shooting starts.
3. **Negotiate passage**: [Persuasion] "Courier neutral. Let me pass."
4. **Pick a side**: Help one faction, make enemy of the other.
5. **Alternate route**: Major detour, significant time cost.

**Outcomes**:
- Wait: Safest, but deadline may suffer
- Push through: Risky but fast
- Negotiate: Works 60% of time, gains neutral reputation
- Pick side: Major faction reputation change

### EVENT D4: CHROME MALFUNCTION

**Tier Range**: 2+ (requires chrome)
**Trigger**: Random, increased after recent installations
**Chance**: 5% base

**Setup**:
*Warning: Chrome instability detected. [Specific chrome] experiencing interference.*

**Manifestations** (varies by chrome type):
- Neural interface: Vision glitches, disorientation
- Speed chrome: Muscle spasms, uncontrolled acceleration
- Sensory chrome: Overwhelming input, temporary blindness/deafness
- Combat chrome: Targeting errors, weapon lockout

**Player Options**:
1. **Push through**: [Endurance] Deliver despite malfunction. Risky.
2. **Emergency shutdown**: Disable chrome temporarily. Lose benefits.
3. **Find mechanic**: Rosa or other chrome tech. Time cost, fix cost.
4. **Abort delivery**: Return package to sender. Job failure.

**Outcomes**:
- Push through success: +Reputation for reliability under pressure
- Malfunction cascade: Chrome damage, potential injury
- Proper repair: Time/credit cost, chrome stabilized

### EVENT D5: VEHICLE PURSUIT

**Tier Range**: 3-7
**Trigger**: High-value deliveries, faction missions
**Chance**: 10% for flagged packages

**Setup**:
*Vehicle behind you isn't just coincidentally going your way. They're matching your turns. They want what you're carrying.*

**Pursuers** (type varies):
- Corporate security vehicle
- Rival courier team
- Unknown faction operatives
- Criminal interceptors

**Player Options**:
1. **Outrun**: [Vehicle/Speed] Pure speed escape.
2. **Lose them**: [Navigation] Complex route through difficult terrain.
3. **Confront**: Stop, face them. Combat or negotiation.
4. **Call backup**: [If contacts available] Request assistance.
5. **Decoy**: [Deception] Lead them wrong way, double back.

**Chase Mechanics**:
- 3 rounds of pursuit
- Each round: player action vs. pursuer response
- Success = distance gained, Failure = distance lost
- 3 successes = escape, 3 failures = caught

### EVENT D6: ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARD

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Weather conditions, infrastructure failure
**Chance**: 15% during severe weather

**Hazard Types**:
1. **Flooding**: Street impassable, find higher route
2. **Electrical storm**: Chrome interference, exposed wiring dangerous
3. **Building collapse**: Route blocked, debris field
4. **Gas leak**: Evacuation zone, long detour required
5. **Network blackout**: Navigation systems offline

**Player Options**:
- **Adapt route**: Accept time penalty
- **Risk it**: [Appropriate skill] Push through hazard
- **Wait**: Some hazards pass quickly
- **Abort**: Return package if deadline impossible

## OPPORTUNITY EVENTS

### EVENT O1: DROPPED PACKAGE

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Random street transit
**Chance**: 3%

**Setup**:
*Something on the ground. A package, clearly dropped. Address label visible. No owner in sight.*

**Examination reveals**:
- Legitimate delivery address
- Package appears valuable
- No identification of original courier

**Player Options**:
1. **Deliver it**: [Good karma] Take to address, potential reward
2. **Open it**: See contents. Determine value. Risk.
3. **Sell it**: Find fence. Gray market profit.
4. **Leave it**: Not your problem.

**Outcomes**:
- Deliver: Small cash reward or contact made
- Open (valuable): Sellable goods or useful item
- Open (trapped): Security measure triggers
- Sell: Quick profit, minor reputation hit
- Leave: No effect

### EVENT O2: INFORMANT APPROACH

**Tier Range**: 2-6
**Trigger**: After completing faction-related jobs
**Chance**: 5% post-mission

**Setup**:
*Someone falls into step beside you. They know your name. They have information to sell.*

**Informant**:
- "Heard you're running for [faction]. I have something they'd want."
- "Information about [rival faction]. Valuable information."
- "I can help you. For a price."

**Information Types**:
- Upcoming raid locations
- Corporate schedule changes
- Safe route updates
- NPC location data

**Player Options**:
1. **Buy**: Pay 100-300 credits for information
2. **Negotiate**: [Persuasion] Reduce price
3. **Intimidate**: [Threatening] Get info for free (consequences)
4. **Decline**: Walk away
5. **Report**: Tell faction about informant

**Value Assessment**:
70% information is genuine and valuable
20% information is outdated or partial
10% information is trap or manipulation

### EVENT O3: SHORTCUT DISCOVERY

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Regular route travel
**Chance**: 8%, higher with navigation skills

**Setup**:
*A gap in the fence you've never noticed. A door that should be locked but isn't. A route that shouldn't exist.*

**Discovery**:
*This shortcut could save significant time on future runs. If it stays accessible.*

**Player Options**:
1. **Use now**: Immediate time benefit
2. **Map for later**: [Navigation] Remember location for future
3. **Share with network**: +Reputation with courier community
4. **Keep secret**: Exclusive advantage
5. **Investigate**: Why is this here? Who else knows?

**Shortcut Types**:
- Building access (may close later)
- Infrastructure tunnel (permanent but risky)
- Timing-based (only works certain hours)
- Patrol gap (depends on security schedules)

### EVENT O4: GRATEFUL RECIPIENT

**Tier Range**: 1-4
**Trigger**: After delivering to NPCs with positive relationships
**Chance**: 15% for repeated positive interactions

**Setup**:
*The person you just delivered to stops you before you leave.*

**Recipient**:
- "Wait. You've been reliable. I want to do something for you."
- "I know people. Let me make an introduction."
- "Take this. You've earned it."

**Rewards Possible**:
- Cash bonus (50-150 credits)
- Contact introduction (new job source)
- Useful item (minor chrome, tool, weapon)
- Information (route tips, faction intel)
- Future discount (services or goods)

**Outcome**:
Building positive relationships creates tangible benefits.

### EVENT O5: ABANDONED STASH

**Tier Range**: 2-5
**Trigger**: Transit through less-patrolled areas
**Chance**: 4%

**Setup**:
*Behind a dumpster. Under a loose grate. The kind of place someone hides things. And someone did.*

**Stash Contents** (random):
- Credits (100-500)
- Gray market goods
- Weapons cache
- Data chips (encrypted)
- Drugs (sellable)
- Nothing (cleaned out)

**Player Options**:
1. **Take it**: Profit, but may belong to someone
2. **Take partial**: Less obvious theft
3. **Leave it**: Not worth the trouble
4. **Stake out**: See who comes back for it

**Risks**:
- 20% chance owner is nearby
- 10% chance stash is watched/trapped
- Taking may create future enemy

## WITNESS EVENTS

### EVENT W1: CRIME IN PROGRESS

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Random street transit
**Chance**: 5%

**Setup**:
*Around the corner, something's happening. Screaming. Violence. Someone needs help. Or doesn't want witnesses.*

**Scenarios**:
1. Mugging in progress (civilian victim)
2. Gang initiation beating
3. Corporate extraction (kidnapping?)
4. Domestic violence spillover
5. Black market deal gone wrong

**Player Options**:
1. **Intervene**: Stop the crime. Combat or confrontation.
2. **Call authorities**: Report anonymously. May or may not respond.
3. **Record**: Document for later use/sale.
4. **Ignore**: Not your business.
5. **Victim extraction**: Help victim escape, avoid perpetrators.

**Moral Weight**:
No right answer. Each choice has consequences, visible or invisible.

---

### EVENT W2: ACCIDENT SCENE

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Random, higher near industrial areas
**Chance**: 6%

**Setup**:
*Vehicle collision. Or construction accident. Or chrome malfunction. Someone's hurt. Emergency services aren't here yet.*

**Scene Details**:
- 1-3 injured individuals
- Varying severity (minor to critical)
- Crowd gathering or absent
- Potential secondary hazards

**Player Options**:
1. **Provide aid**: [Medical/First Aid] Help injured
2. **Call emergency**: Report and wait for response
3. **Assist evacuation**: Help move injured to safety
4. **Continue on**: Deadline doesn't wait
5. **Secure scene**: Prevent secondary accidents

**Outcomes**:
- Aid provided: +Karma, potential contact, time cost
- Emergency called: Moderate +Karma, small time cost
- Ignored: No penalty, but player knows

### EVENT W3: CORPORATE MISCONDUCT

**Tier Range**: 3-7
**Trigger**: Uptown or corporate-adjacent areas
**Chance**: 4%

**Setup**:
*Through an office window. Around a corner. Something you weren't supposed to see. Corporate doing something very wrong.*

**Witnessed**:
- Forced integration procedure
- Unauthorized fork creation
- Evidence destruction
- Violence against employee
- Illegal experimentation

**Player Options**:
1. **Document**: [Stealth/Tech] Record evidence
2. **Intervene**: Directly stop the action
3. **Report to authorities**: (May be ignored)
4. **Report to Resistance**: (Will use information)
5. **Ignore**: Some things you can't unsee
6. **Blackmail**: Use for personal gain

### EVENT W4: FORK CRISIS

**Tier Range**: 4-7
**Trigger**: Any area
**Chance**: 3%

**Setup**:
*Someone standing at the edge. Or pointing something at themselves. Or just... frozen. A fork, overwhelmed by existence.*

**Crisis Types**:
- Existential breakdown (can't accept being a copy)
- Original rejection (creator wants them terminated)
- Identity confusion (multiple memory conflicts)
- Merge fear (upcoming unwanted merge)

**Player Options**:
1. **Talk them down**: [Empathy/Persuasion] Crisis intervention
2. **Call support**: Contact Dr. Vance or fork support network
3. **Restrain**: Physically prevent self-harm
4. **Stay with them**: Just be present
5. **Walk away**: Not equipped to help

**Outcomes**:
- Successful intervention: +Karma, contact made, emotional weight
- Support called: Professional help arrives
- Failure: Witness tragedy

## SOCIAL EVENTS

### EVENT S1: COURIER CHALLENGE

**Tier Range**: 1-5
**Trigger**: Encounter with rival courier on route
**Chance**: 10%

**Setup**:
*Another courier. Same direction. Knows you. Competitive energy.*

**Challenger**:
- "Same destination? Let's make it interesting."
- "First one there gets bragging rights."
- "I've heard you're fast. Prove it."

**Challenge Types**:
1. **Speed race**: First to destination wins
2. **Efficiency race**: Best route, not just fastest
3. **Style challenge**: Most impressive delivery
4. **Endurance challenge**: Multiple stops, who finishes first

**Player Options**:
1. **Accept**: Race begins
2. **Decline politely**: No change
3. **Propose different stakes**: Credits on the line
4. **Sabotage**: [Deception] Mislead them during race
5. **Team up**: Suggest cooperation instead

**Outcomes**:
- Win: +Reputation, possible rival friendship
- Lose: -Reputation, but can be graceful
- Cheat discovered: Major reputation loss

### EVENT S2: OLD FRIEND ENCOUNTER

**Tier Range**: 2+
**Trigger**: Random, based on relationship history
**Chance**: 5% with established NPCs

**Setup**:
*Someone you know. Haven't seen them in a while. They're clearly in the middle of something.*

**Encounter Types**:
- Former contact in trouble
- Mentor checking on progress
- Old rival with news
- Friend from before courier life

**Interaction**:
Brief catch-up conversation. May lead to:
- Side quest hook
- Information about upcoming events
- Request for help
- Warning about danger

**Player Options**:
- Stop and talk (time cost, relationship bonus)
- Quick acknowledgment (minimal impact)
- Pretend not to see them (relationship hit)
- Help with their immediate problem (significant time, major relationship boost)

### EVENT S3: LOST TOURIST

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Transit through any area
**Chance**: 4%

**Setup**:
*Clearly not from around here. Lost, confused, maybe scared. Looking at you for help.*

**Tourist Types**:
- Corporate visitor to wrong district
- Migrant new to city
- Elderly resident in unfamiliar area
- Child separated from guardian

**Tourist**:
- "Excuse me... can you help me find...?"
- "I think I'm lost. Everything looks the same."
- "I'm looking for [location]. Is it far?"

**Player Options**:
1. **Give directions**: Basic help, brief time cost
2. **Escort them**: Major time cost, guaranteed safety
3. **Point vaguely**: Minimal help, no time cost
4. **Ignore**: Not your problem
5. **Exploit**: [Dark] Rob or mislead them

**Outcomes**:
- Escort: Potential reward, +Karma, major time loss
- Directions: Small +Karma, minor time loss
- Exploit: Credits but significant -Karma

### EVENT S4: STREET PERFORMER

**Tier Range**: All
**Trigger**: Transit through market/public areas
**Chance**: 8%

**Setup**:
*Music or art or something that catches attention. A street performer actually worth stopping for.*

**Performance Types**:
- Musician (sometimes with Algorithm-themed lyrics)

20% of performers have useful information or future quest connections.

### EVENT S5: FACTION RECRUITER

**Tier Range**: 2-5
**Trigger**: After gaining visibility in courier community
**Chance**: 6% per visible faction job

**Setup**:
*They approach casually but their intent is clear. A faction wants to have a conversation about your future.*

**Recruiter Types**:
- Convergence evangelist (peaceful, confident)
- Resistance operative (cautious, passionate)
- Corporate headhunter (professional, offering benefits)
- Third Path seeker (philosophical, questioning)

**Approach**:
- "You've been doing good work. Have you thought about doing it for a cause?"
- "Your skills are impressive. [Faction] could use someone like you."
- "There's more to life than packages. Let me tell you about a bigger picture."

**Player Options**:
1. **Listen**: Hear full pitch, no commitment
2. **Express interest**: Opens faction questline
3. **Decline politely**: No relationship change
4. **Decline firmly**: They stop approaching
5. **Play factions**: Express interest in multiple to learn more

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Trigger Frequency
- Average 1 event per 4-5 standard deliveries
- Higher-tier players see more complex events
- Faction alignment affects event types
- Time of day affects danger vs. social events

### Event Chaining
Some events can lead to others:
- Mugging → Contact with underground
- Informant → Future betrayal or loyalty
- Shortcut → Later complications when it closes
- Witness → Future quest hooks

### Save/Load Considerations
Event outcomes should be tracked:
- Shortcuts discovered
- Relationships formed
- Enemies made
- Karma accumulated

### Voice Requirements
- 20 unique event scenarios
- ~60 NPC voice lines
- Multiple outcome variations
- Should feel organic, not scripted
