# Procedural Client Profiles

## Purpose
30 client archetypes for procedural job generation. Each profile provides personality, speech patterns, and interaction flavor to make randomized deliveries feel personal.

---

## CLIENT PROFILE FORMAT

Each client includes:
- **Archetype Name**: Quick identifier
- **Tier Range**: When they appear
- **Description**: Visual/behavioral summary
- **Speech Pattern**: How they talk
- **Job Briefing Line**: What they say when giving work
- **Handoff Line**: What they say at pickup
- **Completion Line**: What they say at dropoff
- **Payment Style**: How they pay
- **Reliability**: Will they screw you over?

## TIER 0-2: STREET LEVEL CLIENTS

### 1. The Nervous Newcomer
**Tier Range**: 0-2
**Description**: First-time client. Sweaty palms, darting eyes. Probably shouldn't be doing this.

**Speech Pattern**: Stammering, over-explaining, apologetic.

**Job Briefing**: "I, uh, I've never done this before. Is this how it works? You just... take things places?"

**Handoff**: "Here. It's not—I mean, it's legal. Mostly. Just... be careful with it, okay?"

**Completion**: "Oh thank god. I mean, thank you. Here's the—wait, is that right? Did I count wrong?"

**Payment Style**: Cash, slightly over-counted from anxiety.
**Reliability**: 9/10 - Too scared to cause problems.

---

### 2. The Street Vendor
**Tier Range**: 0-3
**Description**: Market stall owner. Weathered hands, keen eyes. Knows everyone's business.

**Speech Pattern**: Rapid, transactional, gossip-peppered.

**Job Briefing**: "Need this to my supplier before he closes. You know Mika? Red Harbor? Course you do, everyone does. Anyway, quick job, good pay."

**Handoff**: "Don't drop it. My whole week's profit in there. Also tell Mika his cousin's looking for him. Unrelated."

**Completion**: "Finally! Mika was about to close. You saved my ass. Here, take a little extra. Buy something nice. Or food. Food's nice."

**Payment Style**: Credits plus free merchandise.
**Reliability**: 8/10 - Honest but chaotic.

---

### 3. The Broke Student
**Tier Range**: 0-2
**Description**: Young, exhausted, running on caffeine and desperation. Probably building something illegal in a dorm.

**Speech Pattern**: Fast, distracted, technical jargon slipping out.

**Job Briefing**: "I need components. Very specific components. From a guy who doesn't do standard delivery. Can you be... discreet?"

**Handoff**: "Okay, so there's the thing. Don't shake it. Don't scan it. Don't ask what it's for. It's for... science."

**Completion**: "Yes! This is exactly—wait, is the seal broken? No, we're good. Here's your credits. Sorry it's not more. Thesis budget, you know?"

**Payment Style**: Minimum possible, apologetically.
**Reliability**: 7/10 - Good intentions, messy execution.

### 4. The Worried Parent
**Tier Range**: 0-3
**Description**: Exhausted, stressed, probably hasn't slept in days. Everything is about the kid.

**Speech Pattern**: Distracted, apologetic, occasionally sharp from stress.

**Job Briefing**: "Medicine. For my daughter. The clinic's out, but I found someone who has it. I just can't leave her alone to get it myself."

**Handoff**: "This is her life, courier. Not being dramatic. Literally her life. Please."

**Completion**: "Thank you. I can't—you don't understand what this means. Or maybe you do. Either way. Thank you."

**Payment Style**: Whatever they have. Sometimes overpays from gratitude.
**Reliability**: 10/10 - Would never risk their child's medicine.

### 5. The Local Drunk
**Tier Range**: 0-2
**Description**: Functional alcoholic. Usually at the same bar. Surprisingly well-connected for someone who can't walk straight.

**Speech Pattern**: Slurred but coherent. Accidentally profound. Overly friendly.

**Job Briefing**: "Heyyy, courier! My friend, my best friend. Listen. I need a favor. Little package. Little delivery. Big friendship points."

**Handoff**: "This is—*hic*—this is important. Don't judge the address, okay? We all got exes. I mean friends. Ex-friends."

**Completion**: "You're the BEST. The absolute best. Drinks on me! Not now. Later. When I have money. Soon."

**Payment Style**: IOUs that sometimes pay off.
**Reliability**: 5/10 - Heart's in the right place, memory isn't.

### 6. The Corner Kid
**Tier Range**: 0-2
**Description**: Teenager running errands for someone bigger. Trying to act tough. Failing.

**Job Briefing**: "Yo. Got a job. My... associate... needs something moved. You down or what?"

### 7. The Retired Courier

**Speech Pattern**: Nostalgic, advice-heavy, occasionally bitter.

## TIER 3-4: ESTABLISHED CLIENTS

### 11. The Corporate Burnout
**Tier Range**: 3-5
**Description**: Mid-level corpo who's given up. Does the minimum. Uses couriers for personal errands on company time.

**Speech Pattern**: Dead inside. Corporate jargon used ironically.

**Job Briefing**: "I need you to synergize this package with the recipient's location. By which I mean take it to my apartment. Don't tell HR."

**Handoff**: "This is... personal effects. I'm not stealing from the company. The company stole my twenties. Fair exchange."

**Completion**: "Excellent work. I'd write you a performance review, but those are also meaningless. Here's actual money instead."

**Payment Style**: Company expense account, generously padded.
**Reliability**: 8/10 - Too apathetic to scam you.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Selection Guidelines
1. Weight common archetypes higher in random selection
2. Use tier-appropriate clients only
3. Some clients can become recurring contacts
4. Allow player choice to remember/prefer certain clients
5. Match client personality to job tone (nervous client + dangerous job = tension)

### Voice Consistency
- Street clients: Casual, varied accents
- Corpo clients: Formal, suppressed emotion
- Underground clients: Guarded, coded speech
- High-tier clients: Confident, weighted words
- Algorithm-adjacent: Unsettling, precise

### Recurring Client Flags
