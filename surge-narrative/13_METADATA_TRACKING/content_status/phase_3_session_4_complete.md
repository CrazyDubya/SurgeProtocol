## Narrative Integration

### World-Building Contributions

**Historical Context**:
- **Red Harbor Ruins**: Establishes pre-corpo society, Partition event origins (60 years ago), AI history
- Shows world wasn't always corpo dystopia (alternative was possible, deliberately destroyed)
- AI Fragment 7 provides living witness to engineered catastrophe

**Social Structures**:
- **Chrome Saints**: Gang as working-class response to corpo abandonment (found family, protection, survival)
- **Hollows Market**: Community vulnerability, corpo predation on poor
- **Lost Courier**: Courier community bonds, Chen as mentor figure

**Economic Systems**:
- **Chrome Saints**: Chrome repo economy (gangs repossess augments from debtors)
- **Hollows Market**: Organ trade legalized through contracts ("voluntary donation")
- **Corporate Espionage**: Industrial espionage market, independent contractors as deniable assets

### Faction Development

**New Factions Introduced**:
- **Zhao-Tech**: Rival mega-corp to Nakamura (bioaugmentation competitor)
- **Chrome Saints**: Fully developed as joinable gang faction
- **Drowned Gang**: Scavenger gang (Red Harbor underwater salvage)
- **Razor Collective**: Rival gang (Chrome Saints enemies)

**Faction Relationships**:
- **Chrome Saints** ↔ **Zhao-Tech/Nakamura**: Gangs do corpo dirty work (strikebreaking, repos)
- **Chrome Saints** ↔ **Razor Collective**: Gang rivalry (territory, respect)
- **Nakamura** ↔ **Zhao-Tech**: Corporate warfare (espionage, competition)
- **Courier Community** ↔ **Corps**: Invisible workers until they see too much (disposable)

### Thematic Consistency

**Core Themes Explored**:
1. **Bodies as Resources**: Hollows Market (organs), Chrome Saints (chrome repos), Lost Courier (bioweapons)
2. **History is Erasure**: Red Harbor Ruins (Partition truth suppressed), corporations rewrite past
3. **No Honor Among Corps**: Corporate Espionage (both sides amoral, player is pawn)
4. **Found Family vs. Complicity**: Chrome Saints (belonging requires criminal participation)
5. **Curiosity Kills**: Lost Courier (Sarah died for knowing too much), Red Harbor Ruins (truth is dangerous)
6. **Individual vs. Structural**: Player agency vs. systemic power (can save some, can't fix system)
