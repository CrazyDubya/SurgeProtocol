- Priority 3: 700-930 lines | 14-19 hours
- Priority 4: 400-685 lines | 8-13 hours
- **Grand Total**: 4,750-5,865 lines | 95-127 recording hours

**Budget Scenarios**:

**Scenario A: Indie Budget ($10,850)**:
- New/amateur talent
- Home studio recordings
- Basic post-production
- **Pros**: Affordable, flexible
- **Cons**: Variable quality, less experienced

**Scenario B: Mid-Budget ($22,000-25,000 - RECOMMENDED)**:
- Experienced non-union professionals
- Hybrid studio/remote recording
- Professional post-production
- **Pros**: Professional quality, reasonable cost
- **Cons**: Requires planning, some compromise

**Scenario C: High-Budget ($31,000-32,000)**:
- Union talent or top-tier professionals
- Premium studio throughout
- Full professional treatment
- **Pros**: Award-quality performances
- **Cons**: Very expensive, longer timeline

**Cost-Saving Strategies**:
1. Remote recording (save 50-70% on studio costs)
2. Batch recording (10-20% discount on day rates)
3. Indie casting (50-80% savings on talent)
4. AI voices for background NPCs (save $3,000-5,000)
5. Reduce line count (cut redundant dialogue, save 10-15%)
6. Staged production (Priority 1 now, others later)

**Production Timeline**: 12-19 weeks (3-5 months)
- Pre-production: 2-4 weeks
- Recording Phase 1 (Priority 1): 3-4 weeks
- Recording Phase 2 (Priority 2): 2-3 weeks
- Recording Phase 3 (Priority 3-4): 1-2 weeks
- Post-production: 4-6 weeks

**Payment Structure**: Milestone-based (10% contract, 40% recording complete, 30% edited delivery, 20% implementation)

**Quality Control Checklist**: 10-point QC system for professional delivery
