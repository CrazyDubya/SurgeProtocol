# The Interstitial - Hidden Location Profile

## Location Type
**Hidden Location** - Off-Grid Space Between City and Network

## Geographic Context

**Position**: Everywhere and nowhere (exists in infrastructure gaps)
**Elevation**: Variable (underground maintenance, between-walls spaces, rooftop voids)
**Size**: Unknown (unmapped, constantly shifting as people find/use/abandon spaces)
**Population**: ~5,000-10,000 (estimate, no census)

**Access Points**:
- **Hidden**: Requires knowledge (oral tradition, trusted introduction)
- **Multiple**: Different entrances for different sections
- **Guarded**: Community protects locations from corpo/Algorithm discovery
- **Red Harbor Primary**: Main access discovered at Tier 5 (Okonkwo's guidance)

---

## Physical Description

### Architecture

**"Building" Type**: The Interstitial isn't built—it's **found**

**Spaces Repurposed**:
- **Maintenance Tunnels**: City infrastructure (steam, power, data), forgotten by planners
- **Between-Buildings**: Gaps in construction (errors, abandoned plans, intentional voids)
- **Rooftop Warrens**: Above-street structures (connected by improvised bridges, hidden from below)
- **Basement Networks**: Below-ground (flooded basements, old subway tunnels, sewers repurposed)
- **Dead Network Nodes**: Abandoned server rooms, cable runs, data infrastructure (offline, hidden)

**Construction Philosophy**: Make no new, use what exists, leave no trace

**Infrastructure**:
- **Power**: Siphoned illegally from city grid (taps hidden, metered to avoid detection)
- **Water**: Rainwater collection, filtered groundwater, stolen from mains (minimal)
- **Lighting**: LEDs (low power), bioluminescent moss (cultivated), candles (backup)
- **Heating/Cooling**: Insulation improvised, body heat shared, layers of clothing
- **Communication**: Offline network (physical cables, no wireless, Algorithm-proof)

**Visual Atmosphere**:
- **Organic**: Nothing planned, everything adapted (nature reclaiming industrial)
- **Layered**: Spaces built over time (history visible in strata)
- **Intimate**: Small rooms, narrow passages (defensible, cozy, cramped)
- **Dark**: Minimal lighting (conserve power, avoid detection)
- **Warm**: Despite poverty, feels lived-in, human (contrast to Uptown sterility)

### Sensory Details

**Sight**:
- Low light everywhere (eyes adjust, darkness feels safe)
- Makeshift furnishings (scavenged, repurposed, functional)
- Graffiti (but meaningful—maps, warnings, art, stories)
- Plants growing (hydroponic gardens, moss, mushroom cultivation)
- People dressed in layers (practical, warm, self-sufficient aesthetic)
- No surveillance cameras (deliberate absence = freedom visible)

**Sound**:
- **Quiet** (but alive): Conversations, laughter, music (acoustic, no electric amplification)
- **Natural**: Water dripping, wind whistling, structural creaks (building breath)
- **Human**: Children playing (rare in other districts), genuine joy
- **Absence**: No machinery hum, no sirens, no corpo announcements (peace)
- **Community**: People talk freely (no surveillance, no fear)

**Smell**:
- **Earth**: Soil from gardens, mold/mildew (damp), concrete dust
- **Cooking**: Real food (not synthetic), communal meals, shared spices
- **Bodies**: Crowded, minimal plumbing, natural human smell (not offensive, just present)
- **Fresh**: Surprisingly clean air (no industrial pollution reaches here)
- **Life**: Growing things, decay, renewal (natural cycle)

**Touch/Feel**:
- **Varied Temperature**: No climate control (hot in summer, cold in winter, layers matter)
- **Textured Surfaces**: Rough concrete, smooth scavenged metal, fabric everywhere
- **Humid**: Underground spaces (damp, but manageable)
- **Close**: Tight spaces (bump into people, closeness normalized)

**Taste** (ambient):
- Air tastes clean (no chemicals, no recycling)
- Food tastes real (not synthetic, actual ingredients)
- Water tastes neutral (filtered carefully)

## Cultural & Social Context

### Demographics

**Population Breakdown**:
- **40% Rogues**: People who went off-grid (escaped corpo, augment debt, warrants)
- **30% Born Here**: Second generation (children of Rogues, never on-grid)
- **20% Visitors**: People with access but live on-grid (dual citizens)
- **10% Guides**: Okonkwo-types (bring people in, maintain secrecy)

**Diversity**: Extremely diverse (anyone can hide here)
- Unified by choice (everyone chose off-grid)
- No ethnic hierarchy (survival requires cooperation)
- Multilingual (necessity, everyone speaks multiple languages)

**Augmentation Levels**: Low (by choice and necessity)
- Most had augments removed (Algorithm-connected chrome is tracking risk)
- Basic augments retained (if offline, if necessary)
- Preference for baseline human (ideology + practicality)
- Chrome-free = safe from repo, safe from tracking

### Power Dynamics

**Who "Controls" Interstitial**: No one (decentralized by design)

**Collective Governance**:
- **Elders**: Respected for experience (Okonkwo, others)
- **Consensus**: Decisions made through discussion (slow, deliberate)
- **Voluntary**: No enforcement (if you don't like it, leave)
- **Mutual Aid**: Resource sharing (mandatory social contract)
- **Defense**: Everyone responsible (security is communal)

**No Hierarchy**:
- Former executives and former workers equal here
- Children respected as much as elders (future = present)
- Contribution valued over identity (what you do, not who you were)

**Threat Response**:
- Corpo discovery = evacuation protocol
- Infiltrator detection = community trial (exile if guilty)
- Resource theft = mediation (usually resolved through discussion)

### Daily Life

**No "Schedule"**: Time flows naturally (no shifts, no deadlines, no ratings)

**Morning** (Whenever you wake):
- People wake naturally (no alarms, no obligations)
- Communal breakfast (whoever cooks, everyone eats)
- Tasks discussed (gardens need tending, repairs needed, etc.)
- Children start learning (taught by whoever knows what)

**Day** (Organic flow):
- Work on communal projects (gardens, repairs, scavenging missions)
- Learning/teaching (skills shared freely)
- Creation (art, music, writing—culture preserved)
- Social time (conversation, play, rest)

**Evening** (Gathering time):
- Communal dinner (largest meal, everyone together)
- Storytelling (oral history, entertainment)
- Music (acoustic instruments, singing)
- Planning (tomorrow's needs, long-term projects)

**Night** (Rest):
- People sleep when tired (no artificial schedule)
- Guards rotate (voluntary, watching access points)
- Silence (respect for sleepers)
- Peace (rare in other districts)

### Cultural Norms

**The Interstitial Code**:
- **No Algorithms**: Algorithm-connected devices banned (security)
- **No Surveillance**: Cameras forbidden (trust or leave)
- **Share Resources**: Food, water, shelter communal (selfishness = exile)
- **Teach What You Know**: Skills are community property
- **Protect the Secret**: Location info is sacred (betrayal = worst crime)
- **Welcome the Lost**: Anyone fleeing system can find refuge (but must prove trustworthiness)

**Social Norms**:
- Use first names only (no surnames, no past identities)
- Don't ask "what were you" (who you were doesn't matter)
- Children are community responsibility (everyone raises them)
- Elders given respect (but not authority)
- Consensus takes time (patience valued)
- Conflict resolved through mediation (violence rare, last resort)

## Gameplay Relevance

### Discovery & Access

**Tier 5: "The Space Between" Quest**:
- Algorithm goes offline (network outage)
- Player navigates without digital assistance
- Okonkwo appears, offers guidance
- Hidden entrance revealed (if player proves worthy)
- Access granted (can return later)

**Entry Requirements**:
- Must be introduced by community member (Okonkwo, later others)
- Must prove not Algorithm-connected (no active tracking)
- Must respect secrecy (NPC tests this)
- Can be revoked (betray trust = banned forever)

### Key Locations Within Interstitial

**1. The Garden** (Central hub)
- Hydroponic food cultivation
- Communal gathering space
- Children play here
- Mediation circle for disputes
- Most socially active area

**2. The Library** (Knowledge preservation)
- Physical books (pre-digital media)
- Handwritten journals (community history)
- Teaching space (skill transfer)
- Maps (hand-drawn, Interstitial layout)
- Cultural archive

**3. The Workshop** (Creation/repair)
- Tool sharing
- Chrome removal/modification (make augments offline)
- Scavenged material processing
- Teaching trades
- Community projects

**4. Okonkwo's Corner** (Mentor space)
- Quiet meditation area
- Where Okonkwo mentors seekers
- Philosophy discussions
- Tea ceremony (ritual welcoming)
- Connection to Solomon (Third Path info)

**5. The Threshold** (Boundary space)
- Between Interstitial and on-grid
- Where visitors transition
- Security checkpoint (community guards)
- Emotional/physical liminal space
- Symbolic importance

**Main Story**:
- **Tier 5: Discovery** - First access, meet Okonkwo

- **Tier 9: Solomon Connection** - Okonkwo brings player to Solomon (Third Path)

## Gameplay Mechanical Notes

### Navigation
- No map markers (learn organically)
- Community members give directions (verbal, landmark-based)
- Easy to get lost first visit (intentional)
- Becomes familiar with time (muscle memory)

### Safety
- No combat here (violence rejected by community)
- Healing available (free, limited supplies)
- Rest area (safe save point)
- No time pressure (can stay as long as wanted)

### Resources
- Food provided (communal, free)
- Basic supplies available (barter system, no credits)
- Skills taught (baseline survival, no chrome)
- Information shared (oral tradition, not databases)
